\opening{Dear Editor,}

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled ``\textbf{The Limited Benefit of Transfer Learning for Cross-Condition Battery Life Prediction}'' for consideration as a regular article in \textit{Neurocomputing}.

Transfer learning (TL) is widely applied to address data scarcity in engineering domains, yet negative transfer---where source knowledge hurts target performance---remains poorly understood. Our study provides the first systematic empirical analysis of when and why TL fails, using battery capacity prediction as a rigorous testbed with 213 lithium-ion cells aged under 71 distinct conditions.

Our key findings, relevant to the broader TL and domain adaptation community, are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Within-domain TL provides statistically significant benefit in only 1 of 6 transfer directions, challenging the assumption that more data always helps.
    \item We identify a \textit{feature importance shift} as the mechanistic driver of negative transfer ($p < 0.03$): when predictive features differ between domains, source knowledge becomes counterproductive.
    \item Target domain uncertainty (inter-cell variability) strongly predicts TL effectiveness ($r = 0.888$, $p = 0.018$), offering a practical pre-screening diagnostic applicable beyond battery applications.
\end{itemize}

We believe this work contributes to the understanding of negative transfer and TL applicability, topics of active interest in the machine learning community. The fair comparison protocol (identical base learners for all methods) and the proposed target uncertainty diagnostic address methodological concerns in TL benchmarking.

All data are publicly available (Figshare DOI: 10.6084/m9.figshare.25975315.v1), and analysis code is available at \url{https://github.com/yjkim0123/battery-transfer-learning}.

This manuscript has not been published or submitted elsewhere. We have no conflicts of interest to declare.

Thank you for your consideration.
